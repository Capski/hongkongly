transportation

Your ultimate guide to exploring the vibrant city of Hong Kong

MTR System

Backbone of Hong Kong Transit

The MTR forms the backbone of Hong Kong's transportation network. The system connects Hong Kong Island, Kowloon, New Territories, and Lantau Island through over 10 lines.

Trains run from 6:00 AM to 1:00 AM daily, arriving every 2-4 minutes during rush hours and 4-6 minutes at other times. Fares range from HK$4 to HK$60 based on distance traveled.

Ticketing Options

Your ticketing options include:

- Single-journey tickets from machines
- Tourist Day Pass (HK$65 for unlimited 24-hour travel)
- Octopus Card (reloadable smart card)

The Airport Express gets you from the airport to the city in just 24 minutes.

All stations feature English signage, color-coded lines, free Wi-Fi, elevators for accessibility, and platform screen doors for safety.

Bus Network

Standard Buses

Double-decker buses operated by KMB, Citybus, and New World First Bus reach areas beyond MTR coverage, including remote villages and beaches.

Fares range from HK$3.4 to HK$45 depending on route length. Use your Octopus Card or have exact change ready.

Route numbers appear on the front, back, and sides of each bus. Press the stop button when approaching your destination.

Night routes (marked with "N") operate after the MTR closes. The top deck offers great city views, especially on harbor crossings.

Minibuses

Green Minibuses

- Follow fixed routes with set fares
- Have numbered stops
- Carry up to 19 passengers
- Display clear route numbers
- Accept Octopus Cards

Flag down green minibuses at designated stops. To exit, press the bell or call out "yau lok" (Cantonese for "getting off") when nearing your stop.

Red Minibuses

- Run flexible routes with variable fares
- No fixed stops (tell the driver where to stop)
- Operate mainly in urban areas and late at night
- Accept cash only

Red minibuses can be hailed anywhere along their route. To exit, press the bell or call out "yau lok" when nearing your stop.

Tips for Visitors

Getting Around Like a Local

- Download the MTR Mobile or HKeMobility app for schedules and planning
- Get an Octopus Card immediately upon arrival
- Save the MTR map to your phone
- Note bus route numbers and final destinations
- Keep small change for red minibuses
